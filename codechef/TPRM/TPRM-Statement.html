Astronaut Cooper jumps into a black hole and and want to send some information to his daughter Murph on the Earth. Now Murph is having a prime numbers list so he decided to send the information basing on some prime numbers.

At first he observed that some of the primes have a pecuilar property.

The number 3797 has an interesting property. Being prime itself, it is possible to continuously remove digits from left to right, and remain prime at each stage: 3797, 797, 97, and 7. Similarly we can work from right to left: 3797, 379, 37, and 3.

 You should help Cooper by finding the sum of his required form of prime numbers less than n.

NOTE: 2, 3, 5, and 7 are not considered to be truncatable primes.

Input

Input contains an integer n.

 

Output

Print the answer corresponding to the test case.

 

Constraints

  • 100 ≤ n ≤ 106

 

Example

Input:
100


Output:
186
